# Design Brief

**Project:** Accidental Truth
**Client:** Ron James
**Document Owner:** T&H WebWorks
**Last Updated:** 02/13/2026
**Version:** v1.0

---

## Purpose

Defines the visual and UX direction for the initial launch of `accidentaltruths.com`. This brief is intended to guide rapid design decisions while preserving thematic clarity, credibility, and conversion focus.

---

## Creative Direction Summary

- **Core mood:** Investigative, urgent, credible, cinematic.
- **Narrative stance:** Documentary-first storytelling with clear informational hierarchy.
- **Experience target:** Fast-loading single-page site with strong mobile readability and prominent film promotion.
- **Conversion intent:** Drive trailer views, audience engagement, and merchandise interaction.

---

## Brand Expression Guidelines

### Visual Tone

- High-contrast, editorial documentary look.
- Dark-forward base palette with controlled highlight accents.
- Typography-driven structure over decorative visuals.
- Use imagery to support narrative proof, not visual clutter.

### Voice and Copy Feel

- Direct, factual, and concise.
- Confident but non-sensational.
- Prioritize clarity for mixed technical/non-technical audiences.

---

## Layout Strategy

| Area | Direction |
|------|-----------|
| Hero | Large headline lockup, short supporting text, clear primary CTA |
| Content sections | Modular vertical blocks with consistent rhythm and spacing |
| Media | Embedded trailers in clean, responsive containers |
| Merchandise | Distinct but visually integrated section |
| Footer | Minimal utility and compliance links with unobtrusive styling |

---

## Typography and Hierarchy

- Use a clean sans-serif stack for readability and modern documentary tone.
- Maintain strong heading hierarchy (`H1` -> `H2` -> body) with clear section breaks.
- Keep line length moderate for long-form synopsis blocks.
- Use bold strategically for key facts/CTA labels, not for dense paragraphs.

---

## Color and Contrast Direction

| Role | Guidance |
|------|----------|
| Background | Dark charcoal or near-black primary field |
| Text | Off-white/high-contrast neutral for legibility |
| Accent | One restrained highlight color for CTAs and key links |
| Dividers/surfaces | Subtle tonal shifts for section separation |

Accessibility baseline:
- Body text contrast should meet WCAG AA minimum.
- CTA states (default/hover/focus) must remain clearly distinguishable.

---

## Imagery and Media Treatment

- Prioritize official film stills, poster art, and approved promotional assets.
- Use edge-to-edge media selectively in hero and spotlight sections.
- Avoid heavy effects that reduce source-media authenticity.
- Ensure video embeds do not dominate initial mobile viewport.

---

## Component Behavior Standards

- **Buttons/CTAs:** Clear primary action style with obvious hover/focus states.
- **Section spacing:** Consistent vertical rhythm to support long-scroll scanning.
- **Embeds:** Responsive wrappers with predictable dimensions.
- **Cards/lists (press or reviews):** Clean, compact format with external-link clarity.

---

## Responsive Design Priorities

| Breakpoint Context | Priority |
|--------------------|----------|
| Mobile (primary) | Readability first, compressed navigation, clean media scaling |
| Tablet | Preserve hierarchy and section pacing |
| Desktop | Increase visual depth without adding complexity |

Mobile-first requirements:
- Keep above-the-fold content focused and lightweight.
- Ensure tap targets are comfortably sized.
- Avoid side-by-side layouts that collapse into unreadable stacks.

---

## SEO and Metadata Design Considerations

- Support clear heading semantics for crawler readability.
- Ensure Open Graph preview looks intentional with chosen hero imagery/title.
- Keep canonical URL treatment aligned to standalone domain strategy.
- Favor concise, descriptive section labels that mirror real search intent.

---

## Accessibility Baseline

- Semantic heading order with no skipped structural levels.
- Keyboard-accessible links, buttons, and embedded controls where supported.
- Descriptive alt text for meaningful images.
- Avoid color-only meaning for actions or status cues.

---

## Design Constraints and Guardrails

- Build speed is a hard constraint; avoid patterns that require heavy custom animation.
- Phase 1 must remain single-page and launch-focused.
- Merchandise integration must remain lightweight (embed only).
- Design system choices should be reusable for future multi-page expansion.

---

## Approval Checklist (Design Phase)

- Visual tone approved (cinematic/investigative direction)
- Hero treatment approved (copy + imagery + CTA style)
- Section hierarchy approved against `06-page-outline.md`
- Typography and color direction approved
- Mobile experience reviewed and accepted for launch quality
